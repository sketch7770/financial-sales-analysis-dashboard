Tell me about your project.
✅ Answer

“I built a Sales, Profit & Customer Segment Analysis Dashboard using Excel and Power BI to analyze business performance across regions, customer segments, product categories, monthly sales trends, and discount impact on profitability.

The goal of the project was to identify:

profitable categories,
high-performing states,
customer buying behavior,
seasonal sales trends,
and the impact of discounts on profit.

I used Excel for data cleaning and feature creation, and Power BI for interactive dashboard visualization and business insights.”

2. Why did you choose this project?
✅ Answer

“I chose this project because sales and profitability analysis is one of the most common real-world business problems. Companies need to understand which products, regions, and customer segments generate profit and which areas are causing losses.”

3. What cleaning did you perform?
✅ Answer

“I checked for:

duplicate records,
blank values,
incorrect data types,
and inconsistent formatting.

I converted:

sales,
profit,
and discount columns into proper numeric formats,
and converted dates into proper date format.

I also created additional columns like:

Profit Margin %,
Month,
and Discount Category
for better analysis.”
4. Why did you not remove negative profit rows?
✅ Answer

“Negative profit represents business losses, which is valuable information for analysis. Removing those rows would hide important business problems, especially while analyzing discount impact and unprofitable categories.”

Excellent answer. Interviewers like this.

5. What were the major insights you found?
✅ Answer
Main insights:
California generated the highest sales and profit.
Technology category had the highest profitability.
Consumer segment contributed the highest sales.
November and December showed peak sales trends.
Higher discounts significantly reduced profit margins.
Furniture category had decent sales but relatively weaker profitability.
6. What business problem does your dashboard solve?
✅ Answer

“The dashboard helps businesses identify:

profitable products,
strong and weak regions,
customer purchasing patterns,
and discount strategies affecting profit.

It supports better decision-making regarding pricing, promotions, and inventory planning.”

7. Why did you use slicers?
✅ Answer

“I used slicers to make the dashboard interactive so users can dynamically filter data by category, segment, and state. This improves usability and allows faster business analysis.”

8. Why did you use a donut chart?
✅ Answer

“I used a donut chart to compare customer segment contribution because it visually shows proportional distribution clearly and is easy for business users to interpret.”

9. Why did you use a line chart for monthly sales?
✅ Answer

“Line charts are best for trend analysis over time. It clearly showed seasonal sales patterns and peak-performing months.”

10. Explain the discount vs profit analysis.
✅ Answer

“I analyzed how increasing discount percentages affected average profit. The analysis showed that while low discounts still maintained profitability, higher discount levels significantly reduced profit and even caused losses in some cases.”

This is one of your strongest insights.

11. Which category performed best?
